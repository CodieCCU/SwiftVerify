# SwiftVerify NO CREDIT CHECK Agreement

## Effective Date: February 4, 2026

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

SwiftVerify is a **NO CREDIT CHECK** tenant screening system designed to provide a "Path to Yes" for prospective tenants. This agreement establishes the terms and conditions under which landlords and property managers may use SwiftVerify services.

### 1.1 What SwiftVerify Is

SwiftVerify is a tenant verification platform that approves or denies rental applications based **EXCLUSIVELY** on:

1. **Identity Verification** - Verification of government-issued identification (driver's license)
2. **Employment & Income Verification** - Confirmation of employment and income through Equifax Work Number or equivalent services
3. **Criminal Background Screening** - Optional criminal background checks per landlord policy

### 1.2 What SwiftVerify Is NOT

SwiftVerify **DOES NOT** and **WILL NOT**:
- Perform credit checks or credit score evaluations
- Pull credit reports from credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- Provide credit history information
- Conduct any form of creditworthiness assessment
- Generate hard or soft credit inquiries

---

## 2. NO CREDIT CHECK COMMITMENT

### 2.1 Landlord Prohibition on Credit Checks

By accepting this agreement, you, the landlord or property manager, **AGREE** to the following:

1. **You will NOT conduct credit checks** on tenants who apply through SwiftVerify
2. **You will NOT require credit reports** from applicants as a condition of approval
3. **You will NOT use third-party credit check services** for SwiftVerify applicants
4. **You will NOT request credit scores** from applicants verbally or in writing
5. This prohibition applies:
   - **BEFORE** tenant selection (during the application process)
   - **AFTER** tenant selection (throughout the tenancy)
   - For initial applications and lease renewals

### 2.2 Understanding Credit Checks

A "credit check" includes but is not limited to:
- Requesting or obtaining credit reports from credit bureaus
- Reviewing credit scores (FICO, VantageScore, etc.)
- Accessing credit history information
- Using third-party tenant screening services that include credit evaluation
- Requesting credit card statements or payment histories
- Running "soft" or "hard" credit inquiries

---

## 3. SWIFTVERIFY APPROVAL CRITERIA

### 3.1 Verification Process

Tenant applications are evaluated using the following criteria **ONLY**:

#### A. Identity Verification
- Valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license)
- Name, date of birth, and address validation
- Photo verification and document authentication

#### B. Employment & Income Verification
- Current employment status
- Employer name and contact information
- Monthly gross income
- Employment stability and history
- Income-to-rent ratio (typically 3x monthly rent minimum)

#### C. Criminal Background Check (Optional)
- Conducted only if landlord policy requires
- National and state criminal record searches
- Sex offender registry checks
- Excludes arrests without convictions
- Complies with Fair Housing Act and local regulations

### 3.2 Approval Workflow

Applications progress through the following stages:
1. **SUBMITTED** - Application received
2. **IDENTITY_VERIFIED** - Identity documents validated
3. **EMPLOYMENT_VERIFIED** - Income and employment confirmed
4. **BACKGROUND_CHECKED** - Criminal background completed (if required)
5. **LANDLORD_REVIEW_REQUIRED** - Manual review if background flags exist
6. **FINAL_DECISION** - Landlord approves or denies
7. **APPROVED/DENIED** - Final application status

**NO CREDIT CHECK STAGE EXISTS AT ANY POINT**

---

## 4. RATIONALE FOR NO CREDIT CHECK POLICY

### 4.1 Business Model

SwiftVerify exists to provide housing opportunities for individuals who:
- Have limited or poor credit history
- Are rebuilding credit after financial hardship
- Are new to the country and lack U.S. credit history
- Have non-traditional employment or income sources
- Can demonstrate current financial stability despite past credit issues

### 4.2 Legal and Ethical Considerations

**Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) Compliance:**
- SwiftVerify does not obtain consumer reports as defined by FCRA
- Landlords using SwiftVerify avoid FCRA compliance burdens
- No adverse action notices required for SwiftVerify approvals/denials

**Fair Housing Act Compliance:**
- Credit-based screening can have disparate impact on protected classes
- Income and employment verification provide objective, non-discriminatory criteria
- Reduces fair housing litigation risk for landlords

**Tenant Rights:**
- Tenants are not subject to credit inquiries that could lower credit scores
- Applicants receive fair evaluation based on current financial capacity
- Privacy is protected by limiting data collection

## 5. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION

### 5.1 Agreement Breach

Conducting credit checks on SwiftVerify tenants constitutes a **material breach** of this agreement.

### 5.2 Remedies and Penalties

Violations may result in:
1. **Immediate account suspension** pending investigation
2. **Permanent account termination** for willful or repeated violations
3. **Loss of access** to SwiftVerify platform and applicant data
4. **Financial liability** for any damages to tenants or SwiftVerify
5. **Legal action** to enforce agreement terms and recover damages
6. **Reporting to regulatory authorities** for FCRA or Fair Housing violations

### 5.3 Tenant Remedies

Tenants subjected to unauthorized credit checks may have rights under:
- Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) - Statutory damages up to $1,000 per violation
- Fair Housing Act - Civil penalties and damages for discrimination
- State consumer protection laws
- Contract law for breach of SwiftVerify agreement terms
